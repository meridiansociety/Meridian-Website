About the Society
01

A social and intellectual space for the motivated and open-minded.

The Meridian Society is a student-run, Ottawa-based speaker forum that brings together curious students, alumni, and professionals to share experiences, perspectives, and their career pathways.

Through presentations, open conversations, and informal gatherings, the Society helps its members orient themselves in an increasingly complex world, by gaining exposure to people and ideas they would not otherwise encounter.

Based in Ottawa, Canada, the Society hosts free speaker events, career pathway talks, and open conversations for students across law, business, policy, and media — a place to encounter people and ideas you would not otherwise find.

mer · i · di · an  /məˈridēən/

A meridian is a fixed point in a shifting world - an axis for orientation, judgment, and action.

We liked the idea of creating a place that helps people orient themselves. Not by telling them where to go, but by exposing them to different paths. The Meridian Society exists to do something simple and intentional: bring the right people into the same room, to listen, to learn, and to ask better questions.

What We Are Not
03

Honest about what
we don't offer.

The Meridian Society is not for everyone, and that's intentional. We won't pretend to be something we're not.

×Not a resume workshop
×Not a career placement office
×Not a political advocacy group
×Not a startup incubator
×Not a closed elite circle
"We cannot promise outcomes. We can only provide access, exposure, and orientation to people and their ideas."

What we offer is rare and simple: a room with the right people, and the willingness to listen. That's the whole of it.
