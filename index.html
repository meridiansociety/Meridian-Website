About the Society
01

A social and intellectual space for the motivated and open-minded.

The Meridian Society is a student-run, Ottawa-based speaker forum that brings together curious students, alumni, and professionals to share experiences, perspectives, and their career pathways.

Through presentations, open conversations, and informal gatherings, the Society helps its members orient themselves in an increasingly complex world, by gaining exposure to people and ideas they would not otherwise encounter.

Based in Ottawa, Canada, the Society hosts free speaker events, career pathway talks, and open conversations for students across law, business, policy, and media — a place to encounter people and ideas you would not otherwise find.

mer · i · di · an  /məˈridēən/

A meridian is a fixed point in a shifting world - an axis for orientation, judgment, and action.

We liked the idea of creating a place that helps people orient themselves. Not by telling them where to go, but by exposing them to different paths. The Meridian Society exists to do something simple and intentional: bring the right people into the same room, to listen, to learn, and to ask better questions.

Who We Gather
02

People worth being
in a room with.

The Meridian Society brings together thoughtful speakers and curious young adults, creating space for engaging presentations, meaningful questions, and conversations that expand academic and professional horizons.

01

Students

Curious, ambitious, and open to where ideas might lead. The core of what we build.

Core ▾

Whether you are in first year or finishing a graduate degree, the Society is built around you. You do not need a specific major or a defined career path — just the willingness to sit in a room with people who have been somewhere you have not yet, and the curiosity to ask them about it.

02

Alumni

Graduates who come back to turn what they’ve learned into guidance for others.

Speakers ▾

There is something clarifying about articulating your own path to a room full of people who are just beginning theirs. Alumni return not to lecture, but to reflect — and often find the conversation as valuable as the students do.

03

Professionals

Those with lived experience across law, business, policy, media, and international work.

Speakers ▾

The professionals we invite are not here to recruit or to pitch. They are here because they remember what it felt like to not yet know which direction to face — and they have something honest to say about how they found their footing.

04

Scholars

Scholars whose work shapes perspective and sharpens understanding.

Guests ▾

Academics and researchers who bring rigour and depth to questions that practitioners sometimes move past too quickly. Their presence shifts the tone of a room — from what works to why it works, and what it means.

05

Across Fields

Law, business, policy, media, international work, and everything in between.

All Welcome ▾

The most interesting conversations happen at the edges of disciplines — where a law student asks a journalist a question neither expected, or where a policy researcher and a business founder discover they are solving the same problem from opposite ends. That is the room we are trying to build.

What We Are Not
03

Honest about what
we don't offer.

The Meridian Society is not for everyone, and that's intentional. We won't pretend to be something we're not.

“We cannot promise outcomes. We can only provide access, exposure, and orientation to people and their ideas.”

What we offer is rare and simple: a room with the right people, and the willingness to listen. That’s the whole of it.

×Not a resume workshop
×Not a career placement office
×Not a political advocacy group
×Not a startup incubator
×Not a closed elite circle
